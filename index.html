Initiative for Dependable Agentic Systems

DAS builds, evaluates, and deploys AI agent systems that work reliably in real-world scientific, enterprise, and campus workflows. We treat agent dependability as a systems engineering problem.

Research

Three thrusts toward dependable deployment

Our research agenda bridges AI agent capabilities with systems-level dependability — not another broad AI center, but a focused technical initiative.

Thrust 1

Agentic Infrastructure

Runtimes, memory management, tool integration, observability, and storage-aware execution for agent systems. We build the reliable foundations that agents run on.

Thrust 2

Evaluation & Trust

Benchmarking agent reliability, auditability, human oversight, and cost/latency/quality tradeoffs. We develop methods for measuring when agents are safe, useful, and deployable.

Thrust 3

Applied Pilots

Real-world deployments in cloud operations, scientific workflows, campus IT, and enterprise knowledge work. Partner-facing pilots that create immediate value and new research questions.

Ecosystem

Positioned to move fast

We combine deep systems research expertise with institutional partnerships and industry traction.

  • Systems research base. Deep expertise in storage, I/O, infrastructure, and AI-for-systems — the right foundation for agentic reliability.
  • Institutional partnerships. First State AI Institute, Data Science Institute, UD IT, and national-lab collaborations already in place.
  • Industry traction. Wasabi partnership for agentic cloud-storage log analysis — a concrete pilot with near-term deliverables.
  • Student pipeline. Spring 2026 Agentic Systems course to feed projects, capstones, and student research fellows into center activities.
Flagship Project

PiPilot — an agentic research copilot

An open-source platform that demonstrates what dependable agentic systems look like in practice. PiPilot serves as both a live research tool and the center's software substrate.

  • Multi-source literature search across Semantic Scholar, arXiv, OpenAlex, and DBLP with automated scoring
  • Cross-project paper memory that turns reading history into reusable local research knowledge
  • Integrated data analysis, publication-quality visualization, and academic writing
  • Extensible skill system for domain-specific research workflows
  • Fully open-source — built to be extended by the research community
